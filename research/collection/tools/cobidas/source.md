# COBIDAS: Committee on Best Practices in Data Analysis and Sharing

Source snapshot: Nichols T.E. et al. (2017). Best practices in data analysis and sharing in neuroimaging using MRI. Nature Neuroscience 20(3): 299-303. DOI: 10.1038/nn.4500.

The Committee on Best Practices in Data Analysis and Sharing (COBIDAS) was convened by the Organization for Human Brain Mapping (OHBM) to produce a community-endorsed checklist for transparent reporting and rigorous methodology in functional Magnetic Resonance Imaging (fMRI) studies. The 2017 Nature Neuroscience paper summarizes the report and lists key recommendations.

Recommendations cover:

- Acquisition: scanner make and model, field strength, coil, sequence parameters, echo time, repetition time, voxel size, slice timing, multi-echo or multi-band parameters, eye tracking and physiology synchronization.
- Subjects: inclusion/exclusion criteria, demographics, IRB approval, data exclusions, motion thresholds.
- Pre-processing: motion correction strategy, distortion correction, slice timing correction, spatial normalization, smoothing, masking, regressors of no interest.
- Analysis: model specification, regressors, basis functions, multiple-comparison correction, cluster-forming threshold, software version with commit hash.
- Sharing: raw data, derivatives, code, statistical maps (NeuroVault), participant-level results.

A companion checklist was published as supplementary material, and is now widely used by journals (NeuroImage, Imaging Neuroscience), funders (National Institutes of Health (NIH), European Research Council (ERC)), and review committees as a starting point for evaluating methodological transparency.

The OHBM Open Science Special Interest Group (OS-SIG) maintains an updated COBIDAS-MRI / COBIDAS-MEEG checklist at https://osf.io/anvqy/.
