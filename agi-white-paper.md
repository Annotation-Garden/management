# The Annotation Garden Initiative: A FAIR Annotation Commons for Brain-Behavior Research

## Executive summary

The Annotation Garden Initiative (AGI) establishes open infrastructure for collaborative, multi-layered annotation of stimuli used in neuroscience research. Building on the Brain Imaging Data Structure (BIDS) Stim-BIDS extension proposal (BEP044) and the Hierarchical Event Descriptors (HED) framework, AGI is the substrate that turns one-off per-paper annotation work into a cumulative public good.

The thesis of this white paper, sharpened from a year of community discussion (see [issue #1](https://github.com/Annotation-Garden/management/issues/1) and [issue #2](https://github.com/Annotation-Garden/management/issues/2)) and grounded in a 99-entry literature corpus (see [`research/collection/`](research/collection/)), is straightforward. The proximal reason naturalistic-stimulus datasets are under-used by mainstream cognitive-neuroscience analytics is annotation poverty, not method sophistication. Once a community-versioned annotation layer exists, the standard analytics that today force labs onto resting-state and tightly controlled paradigms become applicable to richly naturalistic data without per-paper annotation overhead. AGI builds the infrastructure that makes the annotation layer possible.

Three direction papers complement this manifesto: [`science-direction.md`](direction-papers/science-direction.md) elaborates the new-science thesis (issue #1), [`tools-direction.md`](direction-papers/tools-direction.md) surveys existing FAIR services and scopes the missing middleware (issue #2), and [`data-direction.md`](direction-papers/data-direction.md) details the dataset rollout, including the cognitive-task wedge that diverges from naturalistic-only framings of the past.

## Annotation poverty as the gating problem

Modern cognitive neuroscience increasingly employs naturalistic stimuli. The Natural Scenes Dataset (NSD) prepared 73,000 Common Objects in Context images for 7T fMRI scanning ([`nsd-fmri`](research/collection/data/nsd-fmri/card.md)). The studyforrest project distributed a 2-hour audiovisual experience across multiple modalities ([`studyforrest`](research/collection/data/studyforrest/card.md), [`studyforrest-eyegaze`](research/collection/data/studyforrest-eyegaze/card.md)). The Healthy Brain Network (HBN) collected EEG and MRI on movie viewing across more than 5,000 participants ([`hbn-mri`](research/collection/data/hbn-mri/card.md), [`hbn-eeg`](research/collection/data/hbn-eeg/card.md)), and the HBN-EEG release powered the NeurIPS 2025 EEG Foundation Challenge.

Each of these datasets represents tens of millions of dollars of acquisition cost. Each generates downstream papers in the dozens or hundreds. And yet most labs that work with naturalistic stimuli pay an annotation cost twice: once when they design their experiment, again when they analyze it. Researchers re-annotate scene boundaries, character changes, emotional valence, semantic content, and acoustic features for the same stimulus that another lab has already annotated, because the annotation is not findable, accessible, interoperable, or reusable in the sense that the Findable, Accessible, Interoperable, Reusable (FAIR) principles ([`fair-principles`](research/collection/tools/fair-principles/card.md)) require.

The cost matters because it shapes which experiments get run. The dominant analytics in the field, voxel-wise encoding ([`huth2016-semantic-maps`](research/collection/science/huth2016-semantic-maps/card.md), [`naselaris2011-encoding-decoding-review`](research/collection/science/naselaris2011-encoding-decoding-review/card.md)), General Linear Model trial-by-trial estimation ([`prince2022-glmsingle`](research/collection/science/prince2022-glmsingle/card.md)), temporal response functions for continuous stimuli ([`mtrf-toolbox`](research/collection/tools/mtrf-toolbox/card.md)), and naturalistic-stimulus encoding at scale ([`antonello2023-scaling-laws`](research/collection/science/antonello2023-scaling-laws/card.md), [`lebel2023-natural-language-fmri`](research/collection/science/lebel2023-natural-language-fmri/card.md)), all require event tables. Resting-state and controlled paradigms produce those tables mechanically. Naturalistic stimuli do not. The path of least resistance is to default to the easier paradigm, which is why labs largely still do resting state even when their hypotheses concern complex sensory experience.

AGI's thesis is that the community-curated annotation layer is the gating piece of infrastructure for the next paradigm shift in mainstream cognitive-neuroscience analytics. The detailed argument lives in [`science-direction.md`](direction-papers/science-direction.md), which walks through the seven Strand C themes from the Phase 1 corpus and shows that five of them are explicitly annotation-richness gated.

## Architecture for collaboration

AGI implements a hub-and-spoke architecture in which each stimulus becomes a versioned Git repository and annotation perspectives live as branches. The "branches as layers" model lets independent annotation efforts (scene boundaries, emotional arcs, object detection, narrative-event annotations) be developed in parallel, then stacked, compared, or merged through standard pull-request workflows. The model is borrowed from open-source software development; Wikipedia is the closest analog from the cultural side, and the [`VISION.md`](VISION.md) document elaborates the metaphor.

Concretely, each repository follows the Stim-BIDS layout ([`bep044-stim-bids`](research/collection/tools/bep044-stim-bids/card.md)):

```
stimulus-name/
├── stimuli/              # actual stimulus files or pointers
├── annotations/
│   ├── visual-saliency/
│   │   ├── events.tsv
│   │   └── events.json
│   ├── emotional-ratings/
│   ├── scene-boundaries/
│   └── speech-transcript/
├── README.md
└── LICENSE
```

Branches stack annotation perspectives. A researcher studying emotional dynamics combines `emotional-arcs` with `scene-boundaries`. A foundation-model team training on HBN-EEG mounts the union of all annotation branches. The bidirectional adapter contract described in [`tools-direction.md`](direction-papers/tools-direction.md) ensures that contributors can keep using their preferred annotation interface (ELAN, Praat, BORIS, Datavyu, Label Studio, or one of the AI-assisted Layer 2 tools) while AGI maintains the canonical versioned form.

## Three flagships, then a second wave, then the cognitive-task wedge

AGI begins with three stimulus repositories that exemplify the platform's value, then expands. The detailed roadmap lives in [`data-direction.md`](direction-papers/data-direction.md).

**Natural Scenes Dataset**. A single repository at `Annotation-Garden/nsd-stimuli` hosts the 73,000 image identifiers and the accumulating annotation layers. The neural data lives in OpenNeuro deposits ([`nsd-fmri`](research/collection/data/nsd-fmri/card.md), [`nsd-ieeg`](research/collection/data/nsd-ieeg/card.md), [`nsd-eeg-alljoined`](research/collection/data/nsd-eeg-alljoined/card.md)) and references the AGI repository by stable identifiers.

**Forrest Gump (studyforrest)**. The 2-hour movie carries an unusually rich set of community annotations developed over a decade: 870 shot boundaries, more than 2,500 spoken sentences with speaker identification, emotion characterizations, semantic-conflict episodes, body-contact events, eye-movement classifications, music timing, and frame-wise perceptual features. AGI's role is to unify these into HED-compatible Stim-BIDS form and to enable additional perspectives. Round-tripping the existing ELAN annotations is Wave 1 of the [`tools-direction.md`](direction-papers/tools-direction.md) build order.

**HBN movies**. The Despicable Me, The Present, and Pixar shorts collection from the Child Mind Institute presents copyright considerations that AGI handles through pointer-based architecture: the AGI repository stores stimulus identifiers and timestamps while annotations reference publicly available copies. The [`hbn-eeg`](research/collection/data/hbn-eeg/card.md) deposit is the existing exemplar for HED Generation 3 sidecars at scale and serves as the proof-of-concept for AGI's quality-control pipeline.

**Second-wave**. [`narratives-pieman`](research/collection/data/narratives-pieman/card.md), [`naturalistic-neuroimaging-db`](research/collection/data/naturalistic-neuroimaging-db/card.md), [`courtois-neuromod`](research/collection/data/courtois-neuromod/card.md), [`things-initiative`](research/collection/data/things-initiative/card.md) and its EEG and MEG siblings, [`bold5000`](research/collection/data/bold5000/card.md), the [`bang-multimodal`](research/collection/data/bang-multimodal/card.md) family, [`sherlock-fmri`](research/collection/data/sherlock-fmri/card.md), [`moth-radio-hour`](research/collection/data/moth-radio-hour/card.md), [`little-prince-fmri`](research/collection/data/little-prince-fmri/card.md). Eighteen entries in the corpus carry the second-wave designation in [`dataset-hierarchy`](research/synthesis/dataset-hierarchy.md). Each becomes an AGI repository when the upstream community is ready to contribute annotations against it.

**The cognitive-task wedge**. The single most important divergence between AGI and prior framings of stimulus annotation is the explicit decision to host cognitive-task batteries alongside naturalistic stimuli. [`hcp-task`](research/collection/data/hcp-task/card.md) (1,200 subjects, seven canonical adult tasks), [`hcp-d-development`](research/collection/data/hcp-d-development/card.md) (1,300 subjects, developmental complement), [`abcd-task`](research/collection/data/abcd-task/card.md) (11,800 subjects, paediatric paradigms), [`cam-can`](research/collection/data/cam-can/card.md) (700 subjects, lifespan multimodal cognitive battery), and [`aomic`](research/collection/data/aomic/card.md) (1,500 subjects, three deposits) together carry combined sample size greater than 16,000 and zero community-curated HED Generation 3 sidecars. The same Stim-BIDS plus HED machinery that handles naturalistic flagships handles cognitive-task batteries; the marginal cost of producing canonical sidecars per task is low and the marginal benefit propagates across thousands of fMRI re-analyses.

## Integration with existing FAIR services

AGI does not duplicate existing FAIR neuroscience infrastructure. The full inventory and adapter-layer scoping lives in [`tools-direction.md`](direction-papers/tools-direction.md). In summary:

- Dataset archive and standards: [`openneuro`](research/collection/tools/openneuro/card.md), [`nemar`](research/collection/tools/nemar/card.md), [`dandi`](research/collection/tools/dandi/card.md), [`bids-validator`](research/collection/tools/bids-validator/card.md), [`bids-spec`](research/collection/tools/bids-spec/card.md), [`bep044-stim-bids`](research/collection/tools/bep044-stim-bids/card.md), [`hed-spec`](research/collection/tools/hed-spec/card.md), [`hed-score`](research/collection/tools/hed-score/card.md). AGI repositories are conformant BIDS plus Stim-BIDS plus HED-tagged datasets. They live alongside the OpenNeuro and NEMAR deposits and reference each other by stable identifier.
- Provenance and versioning: [`datalad`](research/collection/tools/datalad/card.md). AGI's branches-as-layers model leans on the DataLad substrate; explicit DataLad commitments and the federation protocol live in a forthcoming governance document.
- Analysis side: [`neuroscout`](research/collection/tools/neuroscout/card.md) is the closest existing precedent for what AGI proposes on the analysis surface. Its scope is fMRI-only and its annotator is [`pliers`](research/collection/tools/pliers/card.md). A NeuroScout-AGI bridge in which AGI annotations become NeuroScout predictors is the obvious near-term collaboration.
- Analysis pipelines: [`eeglab`](research/collection/tools/eeglab/card.md), [`mne-python`](research/collection/tools/mne-python/card.md), [`fmriprep`](research/collection/tools/fmriprep/card.md), [`mriqc`](research/collection/tools/mriqc/card.md), [`glmsingle`](research/collection/tools/glmsingle/card.md), [`unfold`](research/collection/tools/unfold/card.md), [`mtrf-toolbox`](research/collection/tools/mtrf-toolbox/card.md). A successful AGI annotation flow ends with a HED-tagged events table that any of these tools consumes without AGI-specific code.

The missing middleware is bidirectional adapters between the manual annotation tools (ELAN, Praat, BORIS, Datavyu, Label Studio) and AI-assisted annotators (CLIP, Whisper, Grounding-DINO, LLaVA-Video, PySceneDetect, CrowdAgent) and the Stim-BIDS plus HED canonical form. Building those adapters is AGI's distinctive technical commitment. Pliers is the orchestration substrate; CrowdAgent ([`crowdagent`](research/collection/tools/crowdagent/card.md)) provides the multi-source quality-control pattern; HEDit (developed by the ANNOTATE R01) is the canonical multi-agent annotation client AGI consumes rather than reimplements.

## Complementarity with the ANNOTATE R01

AGI is open community infrastructure. The ANNOTATE R01 (`R01/2026-nlm-annotation/submission/specific-aims.tex` in the internal grant-proposals repository) is a five-year research project led by an investigator team. The two projects are complementary, not competing, and the parallel construction of empirical findings (ANNOTATE) and durable infrastructure (AGI) is the single most important strategic argument for both. The detailed comparison lives in [`scope-diagram.md`](research/synthesis/scope-diagram.md).

In short: ANNOTATE produces empirical annotation-response curves on NSD, HBN-EEG, and Forrest Gump across EEG, iEEG, and fMRI; AGI hosts the resulting annotation layers as versioned repositories and extends the work to datasets and modalities that ANNOTATE cannot reach within a five-year three-modality three-dataset grant. The HEDit annotation client developed by ANNOTATE is consumed by AGI as a first-class input format. AGI's stimulus repositories are ANNOTATE's distribution layer. When ANNOTATE Aim 1 releases NSD HED Generation 3 sidecars at Level 5, they land in `Annotation-Garden/nsd-stimuli` as a versioned annotation branch. When Aim 2 releases the five richness levels, they land as five branches.

## Quality, provenance, and credit

Every AGI repository runs a Continuous Integration (CI) pipeline that validates BIDS compliance via [`bids-validator`](research/collection/tools/bids-validator/card.md), validates HED compliance via the HED-validator integration, and computes inter-source agreement metrics in the multi-rater case. The CI workflow is the technical floor; the social architecture is the GitHub pull-request model that ties every annotation contribution to a contributor record. Credit attribution flows from the per-PR record. Annotation deprecation, fork etiquette, version sunset, and federation tier policy are governance commitments that this white paper names and that a forthcoming governance manifesto will specify.

## What this white paper does not cover

Specific governance documents (FAIR commons governance, federation protocol for restricted-stimulus datasets, credit-attribution policy) are flagged in the Phase 2 [`gap-analysis`](research/synthesis/gap-analysis.md) and are explicitly out of scope here. The technical adapter specifications (ELAN to Stim-BIDS, Whisper to HED-LANG, CrowdAgent quality control on HBN movies) are scoped in [`tools-direction.md`](direction-papers/tools-direction.md) but their per-adapter implementation specifications live in a forthcoming `tooling/` directory. The detailed scientific case for annotation-driven new science lives in [`science-direction.md`](direction-papers/science-direction.md). The dataset rollout phasing lives in [`data-direction.md`](direction-papers/data-direction.md).

## Conclusion

AGI transforms stimulus annotation from a per-paper preparatory step into a cumulative scientific contribution. The infrastructure is concrete: per-stimulus Git repositories, Stim-BIDS plus HED conformance, branches as annotation perspectives, bidirectional adapters to existing annotation tools, integration with OpenNeuro, NEMAR, NeuroScout, DataLad, EEGLAB, MNE-Python, and fMRIPrep. The thesis is concrete: rich annotations of naturalistic stimuli are the gating variable for the next paradigm shift in cognitive-neuroscience analytics, and the same machinery that handles naturalistic stimuli also handles cognitive-task batteries that today have no community-curated HED sidecars.

A garden requires cultivation. The same is true of an annotation commons. The white paper lays out the substrate; the direction papers ([`science-direction.md`](direction-papers/science-direction.md), [`tools-direction.md`](direction-papers/tools-direction.md), [`data-direction.md`](direction-papers/data-direction.md)) lay out the cultivation. The Phase 1 corpus at [`research/collection/`](research/collection/) and the Phase 2 syntheses at [`research/synthesis/`](research/synthesis/) provide the literature foundation. Contributors who want to plant their first row should start with the [`README`](README.md) and the [contribution guide](CONTRIBUTING.md).
