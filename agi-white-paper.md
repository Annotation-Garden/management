# The Annotation Garden Initiative: A FAIR Annotation Commons for Brain-Behavior Research

## Abstract

The Annotation Garden Initiative (AGI) is open infrastructure for collaborative, multi-layered annotation of stimuli used in neuroscience research. AGI builds on the Brain Imaging Data Structure (BIDS), its Stim-BIDS extension proposal (BEP044), and the Hierarchical Event Descriptors (HED) framework to turn one-off per-paper annotation work into a cumulative public good. The thesis advanced in this paper, sharpened from the project's founding scoping discussions[^scoping] and grounded in the 99-entry Phase 1 literature corpus at [`research/collection/`](research/collection/), is that annotation duplication imposes an operational tax on naturalistic-stimulus and cognitive-task research, and that a community-versioned, FAIR-compliant annotation commons amortizes that tax so the value compounds across labs and modalities. A stronger reading — that annotation poverty is itself the gating variable for the next paradigm shift in mainstream cognitive-neuroscience analytics — is plausible but unproven, and is held in this paper as a hypothesis to be tested empirically rather than as a load-bearing claim. AGI is hosted at the Swartz Center for Computational Neuroscience (SCCN) at the University of California San Diego, extends the existing NEMAR / Neuroscience Gateway / San Diego Supercomputer Center stack rather than rebuilding it, and is positioned for support through the National Institutes of Health BRAIN Initiative R24 mechanism (RFA-MH-25-110). Three companion direction papers elaborate the position: [`science-direction.md`](direction-papers/science-direction.md) develops the new-science thesis, [`tools-direction.md`](direction-papers/tools-direction.md) surveys the existing Findable, Accessible, Interoperable, Reusable (FAIR) services and scopes the missing middleware, and [`data-direction.md`](direction-papers/data-direction.md) details the dataset rollout including the cognitive-task wedge that diverges from naturalistic-only framings of the past. The Annotation Garden Archive Product Requirements Document ([`archive-prd.md`](archive-prd.md)) specifies the engineering substrate.

## 1. Introduction

### 1.1 The naturalistic turn in cognitive neuroscience

Cognitive neuroscience has spent two decades shifting from tightly controlled laboratory paradigms toward naturalistic stimuli. The shift began with the discovery that movie viewing produces stimulus-locked synchrony across subjects, opening continuous-stimulus alternatives to block and event-related designs ([`hasson2004-intersubject-synchronization`](research/collection/science/hasson2004-intersubject-synchronization/card.md)). It accelerated through deep-sampling single-subject fMRI ([`huth2016-semantic-maps`](research/collection/science/huth2016-semantic-maps/card.md)), the release of large naturalistic datasets such as the Natural Scenes Dataset ([`nsd-fmri`](research/collection/data/nsd-fmri/card.md)), the studyforrest 7T dataset ([`studyforrest`](research/collection/data/studyforrest/card.md)), and the Healthy Brain Network EEG corpus that powered the NeurIPS 2025 EEG Foundation Challenge ([`hbn-eeg`](research/collection/data/hbn-eeg/card.md)). Each dataset represents tens of millions of dollars of acquisition cost and generates dozens to hundreds of derivative papers. The field has voted with its grants and its publications: naturalistic stimuli matter scientifically.

### 1.2 The annotation cost asymmetry

The methodologies used to analyze naturalistic datasets vary widely in their reliance on stimulus annotation. Some labs lean heavily on programmatic extractors — Contrastive Language-Image Pretraining (CLIP) embeddings, Whisper transcripts, scene-boundary detectors, language-model features — and have published productive encoding analyses on this basis ([`huth2016-semantic-maps`](research/collection/science/huth2016-semantic-maps/card.md), [`antonello2023-scaling-laws`](research/collection/science/antonello2023-scaling-laws/card.md), [`lebel2023-natural-language-fmri`](research/collection/science/lebel2023-natural-language-fmri/card.md)). Other labs publish inter-subject correlation, hyperalignment, or hidden-Markov-model event segmentation that requires no annotation at all. The annotation cost asymmetry that AGI addresses is not the absence of all annotation; it is the duplication of annotation across labs working on the same stimuli, the lack of cross-lab interoperability when annotation perspectives differ, and the operational friction of joining annotations to brain data when each lab maintains its own private representation.

Each naturalistic-stimulus encoding analysis pays an annotation cost twice, once when designing the experiment and once when analyzing it. The annotations are bespoke to the paper, undocumented, lab-specific, and frequently discarded after publication. Researchers re-annotate scene boundaries, character changes, emotional valence, semantic content, and acoustic features for the same stimuli that other labs have already annotated, because the prior annotations are not findable, accessible, interoperable, or reusable in the FAIR sense ([`fair-principles`](research/collection/tools/fair-principles/card.md)). Whether removing this duplication will substantially shift the field's methodological mix toward richer naturalistic designs is an empirical question that AGI's eventual adoption rates will help answer; AGI's *operational* value — annotations citable, joinable, forkable, and accumulating — is established independent of the answer.

The dominant analytics in the field, voxel-wise encoding ([`huth2016-semantic-maps`](research/collection/science/huth2016-semantic-maps/card.md), [`naselaris2011-encoding-decoding-review`](research/collection/science/naselaris2011-encoding-decoding-review/card.md)), General Linear Model trial-by-trial estimation ([`prince2022-glmsingle`](research/collection/science/prince2022-glmsingle/card.md)), temporal response functions for continuous stimuli ([`mtrf-toolbox`](research/collection/tools/mtrf-toolbox/card.md)), and naturalistic-stimulus encoding at scale ([`antonello2023-scaling-laws`](research/collection/science/antonello2023-scaling-laws/card.md), [`lebel2023-natural-language-fmri`](research/collection/science/lebel2023-natural-language-fmri/card.md)), all benefit from per-stimulus event tables when those tables exist. Resting-state and controlled paradigms produce them mechanically; naturalistic stimuli do not, and the labs that produce them privately do not share them. AGI is a community-curated public alternative.

### 1.3 The infrastructural gap

The neuroinformatics community has invested heavily in standardizing the rest of the lifecycle. BIDS ([`bids-spec`](research/collection/tools/bids-spec/card.md)) defines dataset organization. HED ([`hed-spec`](research/collection/tools/hed-spec/card.md)) defines the controlled annotation vocabulary. OpenNeuro ([`openneuro`](research/collection/tools/openneuro/card.md)) and NEMAR ([`nemar`](research/collection/tools/nemar/card.md)) provide the public archives. DataLad ([`datalad`](research/collection/tools/datalad/card.md)) handles versioned distribution. NeuroScout ([`neuroscout`](research/collection/tools/neuroscout/card.md)) demonstrates that automated stimulus annotation plus BIDS pipelines yields reproducible naturalistic-stimulus encoding analyses on fMRI. What no project today provides is a community-versioned, FAIR-compliant annotation layer that the analysis pipelines can subscribe to without each paper re-annotating its stimuli from scratch.

### 1.4 Thesis and structure

This paper argues that a community-curated annotation layer is operationally load-bearing infrastructure for naturalistic-stimulus and cognitive-task neuroscience, and that AGI — hosted at the Swartz Center for Computational Neuroscience (SCCN) at the University of California San Diego, extending the NEMAR / Neuroscience Gateway / SDSC stack — is the body best positioned to build it. Section 2 reviews the prior-work landscape on standards, archives, analysis platforms, and annotation tools. Section 3 specifies the AGI architecture. Section 4 describes the coverage strategy from current flagships through the second wave to the cognitive-task wedge. Section 5 details integration with the existing FAIR services. Section 6 specifies operational complementarity with the ANNOTATE R01. Section 7 specifies the quality, provenance, and credit infrastructure. Section 8 specifies the institutional context, funding strategy, and integrated annotation-driven analyses capability. Section 9 discusses interpretation, limitations, scope, and future work. Section 10 concludes. The engineering substrate of the archive lives in [`archive-prd.md`](archive-prd.md).

## 2. Background

The neuroinformatics commons that AGI joins consists of five layers documented in the Phase 2 [`tool-ontology`](research/synthesis/tool-ontology.md): standards, infrastructure platforms, analysis pipelines, AI-assisted annotators, and manual annotation tools.

The standards layer is mature. BIDS specifies dataset organization and is supported by a reference validator ([`bids-validator`](research/collection/tools/bids-validator/card.md)) and a wide ecosystem of BIDS Apps ([`fmriprep`](research/collection/tools/fmriprep/card.md), [`mriqc`](research/collection/tools/mriqc/card.md)). BEP044 Stim-BIDS ([`bep044-stim-bids`](research/collection/tools/bep044-stim-bids/card.md)) extends BIDS to stand-alone stimulus repositories, which is the architectural primitive AGI inherits. HED provides the controlled vocabulary, with library-schema extensions including HED-SCORE for clinical EEG ([`hed-score`](research/collection/tools/hed-score/card.md)). The FAIR principles articulate the governance rubric.

The infrastructure-platform layer is also mature. OpenNeuro is the canonical public BIDS archive with Creative Commons Zero defaults; NEMAR is the EEG, MEG, and iEEG gateway with HED-tag search and a high-performance computing path; DANDI ([`dandi`](research/collection/tools/dandi/card.md)) is the BRAIN Initiative archive for cellular neurophysiology. DataLad provides the cross-cutting provenance substrate. NeuroScout demonstrates an end-to-end web platform that auto-annotates naturalistic fMRI stimuli with [`pliers`](research/collection/tools/pliers/card.md) and runs BIDS pipelines.

The analysis-pipeline layer covers EEG ([`eeglab`](research/collection/tools/eeglab/card.md), [`mne-python`](research/collection/tools/mne-python/card.md), [`unfold`](research/collection/tools/unfold/card.md)), MEG ([`mne-python`](research/collection/tools/mne-python/card.md)), iEEG ([`mne-python`](research/collection/tools/mne-python/card.md)), and fMRI ([`fmriprep`](research/collection/tools/fmriprep/card.md), [`glmsingle`](research/collection/tools/glmsingle/card.md), [`mtrf-toolbox`](research/collection/tools/mtrf-toolbox/card.md)) with mature BIDS support and partial HED support.

The AI-assisted annotator layer has expanded rapidly. Foundation models for vision ([`clip`](research/collection/tools/clip/card.md), [`grounding-dino`](research/collection/tools/grounding-dino/card.md)), speech ([`whisper`](research/collection/tools/whisper/card.md)), and video ([`llava-video`](research/collection/tools/llava-video/card.md)) produce annotations at unprecedented throughput. Multi-agent orchestration patterns ([`crowdagent`](research/collection/tools/crowdagent/card.md)) coordinate model outputs with human review. Pliers wraps heterogeneous extractors behind a unified abstraction.

The manual-annotation layer is the oldest. ELAN ([`elan`](research/collection/tools/elan/card.md)), Praat ([`praat`](research/collection/tools/praat/card.md)), BORIS ([`boris`](research/collection/tools/boris/card.md)), Datavyu ([`datavyu`](research/collection/tools/datavyu/card.md)), and Label Studio ([`label-studio`](research/collection/tools/label-studio/card.md)) collectively host most of the existing community investment in stimulus annotations. None writes Stim-BIDS or HED natively.

The gap that no current layer fills is the contract between the AI-assisted annotators (Layer 2 in [`tool-ontology`](research/synthesis/tool-ontology.md)), the manual annotators (Layer 1), and the canonical Stim-BIDS plus HED form. Each pipeline that ships an annotated dataset to OpenNeuro or NEMAR re-implements the conversion glue from scratch. The Phase 2 [`gap-analysis`](research/synthesis/gap-analysis.md) names eight concrete gaps; six belong to AGI's distinctive scope.

## 3. AGI architecture

AGI implements a hub-and-spoke architecture. Each stimulus or stimulus collection becomes a versioned Git repository configured as a stand-alone Stim-BIDS dataset, and annotation perspectives live as branches. The "branches as layers" model lets independent annotation efforts (scene boundaries, emotional arcs, object detection, narrative-event annotations) be developed in parallel and then stacked, compared, or merged through standard pull-request workflows. The model is borrowed from open-source software development; Wikipedia is the closest cultural analog, and the [`VISION.md`](VISION.md) document elaborates the metaphor.

Each repository follows the Stim-BIDS layout, in which `stim-<label>` identifies a stimulus and `annot-<label>` identifies an annotation perspective on that stimulus, both as first-class BIDS entities ([`bep044-stim-bids`](research/collection/tools/bep044-stim-bids/card.md)):

```
stimulus-repo/
├── dataset_description.json
├── README
├── LICENSE
└── stimuli/
    ├── stimuli.tsv                              # stimulus index keyed by stimulus_id
    ├── stimuli.json
    ├── annotations.tsv                          # annotation index keyed by annot_id
    ├── annotations.json
    ├── stim-<label>_<suffix>.<ext>              # stimulus file; suffix is image, audio, video, or audiovideo
    ├── stim-<label>_<suffix>.json               # per-stimulus metadata (license, copyright, URL, description)
    ├── stim-<label>_annot-<label>_events.tsv    # time-varying annotation, inheriting the BIDS events.tsv structure
    └── stim-<label>_annot-<label>_events.json   # column descriptors with HED tags
```

For static-image stimuli (e.g., NSD), per-image annotations are carried as columns in `stimuli.tsv` and as fields in the `stim-<label>_image.json` sidecar, with HED tags inline; for time-varying stimuli (e.g., studyforrest), each annotation perspective is a separate `stim-<label>_annot-<label>_events.tsv` file. Branches stack annotation perspectives. A researcher studying emotional dynamics combines the `emotional-arcs` branch with the `scene-boundaries` branch. A foundation-model team training on HBN-EEG mounts the union of all annotation branches. The bidirectional adapter contract described in [`tools-direction.md`](direction-papers/tools-direction.md) ensures that contributors keep using their preferred annotation interface (ELAN, Praat, BORIS, Datavyu, Label Studio, or one of the Layer 2 AI-assisted tools) while AGI maintains the canonical versioned form.

## 4. Coverage strategy

AGI begins with three stimulus repositories that exemplify the platform's value, expands to second-wave naturalistic datasets, and explicitly covers cognitive-task batteries that prior framings of stimulus annotation have left aside. The detailed roadmap lives in [`data-direction.md`](direction-papers/data-direction.md).

**Natural Scenes Dataset.** A single repository at `Annotation-Garden/nsd-stimuli` hosts the 73,000 image identifiers and the accumulating annotation layers. The neural data lives in OpenNeuro deposits ([`nsd-fmri`](research/collection/data/nsd-fmri/card.md), [`nsd-ieeg`](research/collection/data/nsd-ieeg/card.md), [`nsd-eeg-alljoined`](research/collection/data/nsd-eeg-alljoined/card.md)) and references the AGI repository by stable identifiers.

**Forrest Gump (studyforrest).** The 2-hour movie carries an unusually rich set of community annotations developed over a decade: 870 shot boundaries, more than 2,500 spoken sentences with speaker identification, emotion characterizations, semantic-conflict episodes, body-contact events, eye-movement classifications, music timing, and frame-wise perceptual features. AGI's role is to unify these into HED-compatible Stim-BIDS form and to enable additional perspectives. Round-tripping the existing ELAN annotations is Wave 1 of the [`tools-direction.md`](direction-papers/tools-direction.md) build order.

**HBN movies.** The Despicable Me, The Present, and Pixar shorts collection from the Child Mind Institute presents copyright considerations that AGI handles through a pointer-based architecture: the AGI repository stores stimulus identifiers and timestamps while annotations reference publicly available copies. The [`hbn-eeg`](research/collection/data/hbn-eeg/card.md) deposit is the existing exemplar for HED Generation 3 sidecars at scale. The HBN movies repository is held to Phase 2 contingent on a written Data Use Agreement from Child Mind Institute and the relevant rightsholders being in hand and on the federation tier of [`archive-prd.md`](archive-prd.md) Section 11.1 being implemented end-to-end on a less legally complex flagship first; this deferral reflects the convergent recommendation of the v0 PRD review.

The Phase 1 launch flagships are therefore studyforrest first (Hanke as collaborator, ELAN round-trip as the canonical adapter test, codec-tolerant fingerprinting as the equivalence-protocol stress test) and the Natural Scenes Dataset second (originator engagement with Kay and Naselaris as a launch prerequisite). The cognitive-task wedge runs as a parallel track with its own dedicated maintainership; localizers and task batteries enter the archive as one-pull-request contributions per stimulus.

**Second wave.** Eighteen entries in the Phase 1 corpus carry the second-wave designation in the [`dataset-hierarchy`](research/synthesis/dataset-hierarchy.md). They span the static-image strand ([`things-initiative`](research/collection/data/things-initiative/card.md), [`nod-eeg`](research/collection/data/nod-eeg/card.md), [`things-eeg2`](research/collection/data/things-eeg2/card.md), [`bold5000`](research/collection/data/bold5000/card.md)), the video strand ([`bang-ieeg-fmri`](research/collection/data/bang-ieeg-fmri/card.md), [`bang-multimodal`](research/collection/data/bang-multimodal/card.md), [`sherlock-fmri`](research/collection/data/sherlock-fmri/card.md), [`naturalistic-neuroimaging-db`](research/collection/data/naturalistic-neuroimaging-db/card.md), [`courtois-neuromod`](research/collection/data/courtois-neuromod/card.md)), and the audio strand ([`narratives-pieman`](research/collection/data/narratives-pieman/card.md), [`moth-radio-hour`](research/collection/data/moth-radio-hour/card.md), [`little-prince-fmri`](research/collection/data/little-prince-fmri/card.md)). Each becomes an AGI repository when the upstream community is ready to contribute annotations against it.

**The cognitive-task wedge.** The single most important divergence between AGI and prior framings of stimulus annotation is the explicit decision to host cognitive-task batteries alongside naturalistic stimuli. Five canonical batteries, [`hcp-task`](research/collection/data/hcp-task/card.md) (1,200 subjects, seven adult tasks), [`hcp-d-development`](research/collection/data/hcp-d-development/card.md) (1,300 subjects, developmental complement), [`abcd-task`](research/collection/data/abcd-task/card.md) (11,800 subjects, paediatric paradigms), [`cam-can`](research/collection/data/cam-can/card.md) (700 subjects, lifespan multimodal cognitive battery), and [`aomic`](research/collection/data/aomic/card.md) (1,500 subjects, three deposits), together carry combined sample size greater than 16,000 and zero community-curated HED Generation 3 sidecars. Three single-stimulus localizer cohorts ([`fedorenko-language-localizer`](research/collection/data/fedorenko-language-localizer/card.md), [`pinel-localizer`](research/collection/data/pinel-localizer/card.md), [`localizer-ds000114`](research/collection/data/localizer-ds000114/card.md)) are natural one-PR contributions per localizer that propagate across thousands of fMRI re-analyses. The same Stim-BIDS plus HED machinery that handles naturalistic flagships handles cognitive-task batteries and localizers; the marginal cost of producing canonical sidecars per task is low and the marginal benefit propagates broadly.

## 5. Integration with existing FAIR services

AGI does not duplicate existing FAIR neuroscience infrastructure. The full inventory and adapter-layer scoping lives in [`tools-direction.md`](direction-papers/tools-direction.md). In summary, AGI inherits the dataset-archive layer, the standards layer, the provenance layer, and the analysis-pipeline layer; it does not inherit the contract layer that turns AI-assisted and manual annotation outputs into the HED-tagged `stim-<label>_annot-<label>_events.tsv` files (and equivalent `stimuli.tsv` columns for static stimuli) that Stim-BIDS specifies. Building that contract layer is AGI's distinctive technical commitment.

Concretely:

- **Dataset archive and standards.** AGI repositories are conformant BIDS plus Stim-BIDS plus HED-tagged datasets that pass the existing [`bids-validator`](research/collection/tools/bids-validator/card.md) and HED validators without AGI-specific patches. They live alongside [`openneuro`](research/collection/tools/openneuro/card.md), [`nemar`](research/collection/tools/nemar/card.md), and [`dandi`](research/collection/tools/dandi/card.md) deposits and reference each other by stable identifier.
- **Provenance and versioning.** AGI's branches-as-layers model leans on the [`datalad`](research/collection/tools/datalad/card.md) substrate. Explicit DataLad commitments and the federation protocol for restricted-stimulus datasets live in a forthcoming governance document.
- **Analysis side.** [`neuroscout`](research/collection/tools/neuroscout/card.md) is the closest existing precedent for what AGI proposes. NeuroScout's scope is fMRI-only and its annotator is [`pliers`](research/collection/tools/pliers/card.md). A NeuroScout-AGI bridge in which AGI annotations become NeuroScout predictors is the obvious near-term collaboration; AGI generalizes the lift to MEG, EEG, iEEG, and to multi-source annotation provenance that exceeds what Pliers alone produces.
- **Analysis pipelines.** A successful AGI annotation flow ends with a HED-tagged events table that [`eeglab`](research/collection/tools/eeglab/card.md), [`mne-python`](research/collection/tools/mne-python/card.md), [`fmriprep`](research/collection/tools/fmriprep/card.md), [`mriqc`](research/collection/tools/mriqc/card.md), [`glmsingle`](research/collection/tools/glmsingle/card.md), [`unfold`](research/collection/tools/unfold/card.md), or [`mtrf-toolbox`](research/collection/tools/mtrf-toolbox/card.md) consumes without AGI-specific code.

The missing middleware is bidirectional adapters between manual annotation tools (ELAN, Praat, BORIS, Datavyu, Label Studio) and AI-assisted annotators (CLIP, Whisper, Grounding-DINO, LLaVA-Video, PySceneDetect, CrowdAgent) and the Stim-BIDS plus HED canonical form. Pliers is the orchestration substrate; CrowdAgent ([`crowdagent`](research/collection/tools/crowdagent/card.md)) provides the multi-source quality-control pattern; HEDit (developed by the ANNOTATE R01) is the canonical multi-agent annotation client AGI consumes rather than reimplements.

## 6. Complementarity with the ANNOTATE R01

AGI is open community infrastructure. The ANNOTATE R01 (`R01/2026-nlm-annotation/submission/specific-aims.tex` in the internal grant-proposals repository) is a five-year research project led by an investigator team. The two projects are complementary, not competing, and the parallel construction of empirical findings (ANNOTATE) and durable infrastructure (AGI) is the strongest strategic argument for both. The detailed comparison lives in [`scope-diagram.md`](research/synthesis/scope-diagram.md).

In short: ANNOTATE produces empirical annotation-response curves on NSD across all three modalities (EEG, iEEG, fMRI per Aim 1) and validates generalization on HBN-EEG (Aim 3, EEG only) and on StudyForrest (Aim 3, fMRI only). AGI hosts the resulting annotation layers as versioned repositories and extends the work to datasets and modalities that ANNOTATE cannot reach within a five-year three-modality three-dataset grant. The HEDit annotation client developed by ANNOTATE is consumed by AGI as a first-class input format. AGI's stimulus repositories are ANNOTATE's distribution layer. When ANNOTATE Aim 1 releases NSD HED Generation 3 sidecars at Level 5, they land in `Annotation-Garden/nsd-stimuli` as a versioned annotation branch. When Aim 2 releases the five richness levels, they land as five branches.

## 7. Quality, provenance, and credit

Every AGI repository runs a Continuous Integration (CI) pipeline that validates BIDS compliance via the [`bids-validator`](research/collection/tools/bids-validator/card.md), validates HED compliance via the HED-validator integration, and computes inter-source agreement metrics in the multi-rater case. The CI workflow is the technical floor; the social architecture is the GitHub pull-request model that ties every annotation contribution to a contributor record.

Credit attribution rests on a per-release `CITATION.cff` file with Contributor Roles Taxonomy (CRediT) roles and Open Researcher and Contributor Identifier (ORCID) identifiers per contributor; the file is a release blocker if contributors have not agreed on author order. Authorship by commit count is explicitly rejected as a default. Each annotation perspective release receives a Git tag, a Zenodo per-version Digital Object Identifier (DOI), and a Zenodo concept DOI; the change-type tag (`additive`, `corrected`, `re-rated`, `consolidated`, `schema-bumped`, `restructured`) describes how the release differs from its predecessor. AGI inherits the dual DOI pipeline used by NEMAR (Zenodo Communities for community releases via `nemar-tools`; the University of California California Digital Library DataCite membership for institutional-grade canonical releases). Per-row provenance columns (`annotator`, `tool`, `tool_version`, `model_checkpoint`, `timestamp`) are mandatory on new annotation files. The Code of Conduct is BIDS-style, based on Contributor Covenant 2.1. Annotation deprecation, fork etiquette, retraction propagation, and federation tier policy are specified in [`archive-prd.md`](archive-prd.md) Sections 10–11 and elaborated in a forthcoming governance manifesto.

### 8.2 Inherited infrastructure

AGI extends rather than rebuilds. SCCN at UCSD operates NEMAR as an Amazon Web Services Open Data Sponsorship resource with `nemar-cli` (the user-facing command-line tool), `nemar-tools` (the publication pipeline that mints Zenodo DOIs and generates metadata), `nemar-metadata` (the per-dataset web-interface index), `neuroschema` (an extensible JSON Schema for neuroimaging metadata), and the SCCN-maintained `NEMAR-pipeline` for processing. The Neuroscience Gateway (NSG) and the San Diego Supercomputer Center (SDSC) provide compute. AGI inherits this stack — dataset-per-repo, Zenodo-via-`nemar-tools`, AWS Open Data hosting, `neuroschema` as the metadata substrate, NSG for compute — and layers the annotation commons on top.

### 8.3 Funding strategy

AGI's primary funding target is National Institutes of Health Funding Opportunity **RFA-MH-25-110**, "BRAIN Initiative: Data Archives for the BRAIN Initiative (R24 Clinical Trial Optional)," issued by the National Institute of Mental Health on behalf of the BRAIN Initiative. The Multi-Principal-Investigator team is Shirazi (contact, UCSD/SCCN), Halchenko (Dartmouth, DataLad), Amit Majumdar (SDSC, NSG), and Robbins (UTSA, HED). The R24 mechanism explicitly funds data archives in BRAIN sub-domains; AGI's sub-domain is **stimulus annotations**, distinct from the brain-data archives (OpenNeuro, NEMAR, DANDI). The application is pending submission for the June 24, 2026 due date. The mechanism's responsiveness discipline shapes AGI's framing: the project is a data archive whose data are annotations, with tools and analyses as integrated capabilities per the NOFO's permitted scope, not primary deliverables. Detailed alignment with the R24's Research Strategy sub-sections (standards and identifiers, data storage and protection, data accessibility, analysis and visualization tools, timeline) lives in [`archive-prd.md`](archive-prd.md) Sections 11.2 and 11.3.

### 8.4 Annotation-driven analyses as integrated capability

The archive is not a static FAIR commons. As an integrated capability — aligned with the R24 NOFO's "Analysis and Visualization Tools" Research Strategy sub-section — AGI dispatches annotation-driven analyses to the Neuroscience Gateway via REST application programming interface, runs them on SDSC compute, and surfaces results in the archive view. The Phase 1 canonical recipes are per-annotation Event-Related Potential (ERP), per-annotation Event-Related Spectral Perturbation (ERSP), and cross-stimulus or cross-cohort grand averages for Electroencephalography datasets that consume an AGI stimulus; General Linear Model regressors derived programmatically from annotation columns for functional Magnetic Resonance Imaging datasets; and event-locked high-gamma broadband power for intracranial Electroencephalography as a fast-follow modality. Each recipe is a first-class identified object with stable identifier, version history, citation, and contributor list, mirroring the data model for annotation perspectives. Each invocation produces a citable job receipt. The canonical Phase 1 demonstration is HBN-EEG ERP locked to per-shot annotations on The Present, grand-averaged across the cohort. Architectural detail lives in [`archive-prd.md`](archive-prd.md) Section 12.

## 9. Discussion

### 9.1 Interpretation

The argument advanced in this paper rests on a conservative reading: AGI lowers the operational cost of naturalistic-stimulus and cognitive-task neuroscience by replacing per-paper annotation duplication with community-curated, versioned, citable annotation layers. The conservative reading is sufficient to motivate the project on its own; the value compounds with each annotation perspective contributed and each dataset that reuses an existing annotation rather than re-annotating.

A stronger reading — that the current dominance of resting-state and tightly controlled-paradigm designs in cognitive neuroscience reflects an annotation-cost asymmetry rather than substantive scientific judgment, and that removing the asymmetry will redirect the field toward richer designs — is held in this paper as a hypothesis to be tested empirically rather than as a load-bearing claim. The Phase 1 corpus does not adjudicate the strong reading. The experiments that would adjudicate it (ANNOTATE Aim 2, plus AGI-hosted replications across second-wave datasets) have not yet been run, and the labs that already produce dense naturalistic-stimulus encoding analyses do so through programmatic extractors that do not principally depend on community-curated annotation. AGI's success criteria — citable annotations, joinable to brain data, accumulating over time — are independent of the strong reading's eventual outcome.

### 9.2 Limitations

This paper is a position document that synthesizes a 99-entry literature corpus into an infrastructural argument. It is not a meta-analysis. It does not estimate effect sizes for the predicted shifts and does not bound the rate at which the community will adopt AGI-hosted annotations. The cognitive-task-wedge claim assumes that HED Generation 3 sidecars on canonical task batteries will see broad uptake; the assumption is plausible because the marginal cost is low and the BIDS-conformant releases of the underlying datasets already exist, but uptake depends on documentation, tutorials, and integration into the standard analysis pipelines that AGI has not yet shipped.

A second limitation is the scope of the corpus. Twenty-three of the thirty Strand C entries are fMRI-primary; only five entries cover EEG or iEEG primary recordings. Tools used heavily in adjacent communities (CVAT, Prodigy, web-based crowdsourcing platforms beyond Label Studio) are not in the corpus. The omissions reflect the historical distribution of naturalistic-stimulus literature and the Phase 1 collection scope rather than oversight.

### 9.3 Scope clarification, what this white paper does not cover

Three classes of deliverable are explicitly out of scope for this manifesto. First, specific governance documents (FAIR commons governance, federation protocol for restricted-stimulus datasets, credit-attribution policy) are flagged in the Phase 2 [`gap-analysis`](research/synthesis/gap-analysis.md) and will live in a forthcoming governance directory. Second, technical adapter specifications (ELAN to Stim-BIDS, Whisper to HED-LANG, CrowdAgent quality control on HBN movies) are scoped in [`tools-direction.md`](direction-papers/tools-direction.md) but their per-adapter implementation specifications belong in a forthcoming `tooling/` directory. Third, the detailed scientific case for annotation-driven new science lives in [`science-direction.md`](direction-papers/science-direction.md), and the dataset rollout phasing lives in [`data-direction.md`](direction-papers/data-direction.md); this white paper points readers at those documents rather than reproducing them.

### 9.4 Future work

The empirical experiments that would test the thesis fall in four categories. First, annotation-richness sufficiency curves at multiple levels per stimulus, replicated across modalities and across datasets, beginning with the ANNOTATE R01's Aim 2 work on NSD and extending across second-wave datasets via AGI replication. Second, annotation-supervised foundation-model pretraining benchmarked head-to-head against unsupervised pretraining on the same downstream tasks, beginning with HBN-EEG. Third, cross-modal alignment papers that share a single HED-tagged annotation layer across two or more recording modalities on the same stimulus, beginning with the NSD triangle. Fourth, interpretive ISC and event-segmentation analyses that decompose shared signal by annotated stimulus features. None of these requires methodological innovation beyond what the field has already validated; each requires the AGI substrate.

### 9.5 Anticipated objection

A reader could argue that the field's preference for resting-state and controlled paradigms reflects substantive scientific judgment about confound control and inferential clarity, and that lifting the annotation cost will not substantially shift the methodological mix. The Phase 1 corpus does not refute the argument directly. It provides circumstantial evidence against it: every theme in the corpus that uses naturalistic stimuli flags its annotation-side limitation in the open-questions section of one or more constituent papers. The cumulative weight of those flags suggests that the community has been actively waiting on the annotation layer; the substantive-judgment alternative would predict less consensus on what the bottleneck is.

## 10. Conclusion

AGI transforms stimulus annotation from a per-paper preparatory step into a cumulative scientific contribution. The infrastructure is concrete: per-stimulus Git repositories conformant to Stim-BIDS plus HED, branches as annotation perspectives, bidirectional adapters to existing annotation tools, integration with OpenNeuro, NEMAR, NeuroScout, DataLad, EEGLAB, MNE-Python, and fMRIPrep, and integrated annotation-driven analyses dispatched to the Neuroscience Gateway. The institutional substrate is concrete: SCCN at UCSD as host, NEMAR / NSG / SDSC as inherited infrastructure, an advisory board drawn from the HED, EEGLAB, NSD, DataLad, and language-annotation communities, and the BRAIN R24 mechanism (RFA-MH-25-110) as the funding pathway. The operational thesis is concrete and defensible: rich annotations of stimuli — naturalistic and cognitive-task alike — are an operationally load-bearing input to brain-behavior research, and a community-curated annotation commons amortizes the duplication cost across labs while making annotations citable, joinable, and forkable on their own terms.

A garden requires cultivation. The same is true of an annotation commons. This white paper lays out the substrate; [`archive-prd.md`](archive-prd.md) specifies the engineering substrate; the direction papers ([`science-direction.md`](direction-papers/science-direction.md), [`tools-direction.md`](direction-papers/tools-direction.md), [`data-direction.md`](direction-papers/data-direction.md)) lay out the scientific, tools, and dataset cultivation. The Phase 1 corpus at [`research/collection/`](research/collection/) and the Phase 2 syntheses at [`research/synthesis/`](research/synthesis/) provide the literature foundation. Contributors who want to plant their first row should start with the [`README`](README.md) and the [contribution guide](CONTRIBUTING.md).
